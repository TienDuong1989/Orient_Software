Author Program · Orient Software · 2026
A proposal to assign
subject-matter experts
as the primary authors
of Orient Software's blog.
This replaces content writers in the author field with engineers, architects, and company leadership — the people with verifiable expertise in the topics being written about.
Why This Matters
Two reasons Orient needs this now
1
Google evaluates content quality through E-E-A-T
One of the clearest signals Google reads is who authored the article — their title, LinkedIn profile, and author page. For technical topics like software architecture or legacy modernization, a content writer carries no verifiable expertise signal. A CTO or senior engineer does.
2
The majority of Orient's blog articles are authored by content writers
This means Orient's articles carry a weaker E-E-A-T signal compared to competitors who publish under named engineers, architects, or company leadership — resulting in lower rankings on technical topics where domain expertise is a key ranking factor.

Recommendation
All new and updated blog articles on Orient Software should be attributed to a domain expert as the primary author. The content team continues to write — the expert contributes their name, title, and credibility. No additional writing work is required from the authors. This will be applied to the AI Native campaign first, then rolled out to all new articles going forward.
→ Requires approval from Marketing Manager This proposal needs sign-off before implementation begins. Once approved, the timeline in Tab 03 will be initiated.
⚠ Action required — Content Team The Content Team must be notified in advance of this change. Going forward, every new article brief should include the assigned author name before writing begins. Content writers continue their work as usual — the only change is that a domain expert's name replaces theirs on the published article.
