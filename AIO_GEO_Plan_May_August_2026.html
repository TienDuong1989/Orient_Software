Executive Summary
Why Orient needs a GEO/AIO strategy now — and what this plan commits to.
B2B buyers are changing how they discover vendors. A CTO evaluating software partners increasingly asks an AI assistant before running a Google search. When they type "best approach for legacy system modernization" or "what is spec-driven development" — Orient needs to appear in those answers. This plan is about building that visibility systematically.
What this plan covers: A structured GEO/AIO execution strategy running May through August 2026 — including citation pattern analysis, prompt mapping, 11 GEO-optimized articles, competitive positioning, and monthly performance tracking. Semrush AI Visibility is the measurement and audit tool that supports the work.
Current baseline (May 2026): 15 AI Mentions — ChatGPT: 3 · AI Overview: 7 · AI Mode: 2 · Gemini: 3 — with 967 Cited Pages. Target: 50 Mentions by end of August 2026. The gap between 967 cited pages and only 15 mentions signals that Orient has content being indexed but is not yet being selected as a trusted citation source by AI.

02
Context & Challenges
The shift in B2B discovery behavior — and the three gaps Orient needs to close.

B2B buyers are increasingly bypassing traditional search and querying AI assistants directly. When a CTO asks "best approach for legacy system modernization" or a CEO asks "what is spec-driven development" — Orient needs to appear in those answers, not just on page one of Google. Three gaps prevent this today.

01
No visibility into what AI is citing
Orient has 967 cited pages but no data on which ones AI actually selects, for which prompts, and why. Without knowing the citation formula, it cannot be replicated.
02
No prompt mapping for the ICP
No data on which prompts Orient's ICP — CTOs and CEOs evaluating software partners — is asking AI platforms, or where competitors are appearing instead of Orient.
03
No GEO standards in the content process
Content is written to rank on Google. GEO optimization — cited statistics in opening paragraphs, direct question-answer structure, FAQ schema — is not yet applied systematically.
03
GEO/AIO Strategy
Four pillars that structure how Orient builds AI citation visibility.
01
Citation Pattern Analysis
Before writing new content, audit what is already being cited. Understand the formula — what structure, what type of claim, what source format makes AI select a page. Apply this pattern systematically to all new and updated articles.
02
Prompt Mapping
Identify the exact prompts Orient's ICP is asking AI platforms. Map these to existing and planned content. Each GEO article is written to answer a specific prompt directly — not just to rank on Google for a keyword.
03
Competitive Positioning
Identify which competitors are appearing in AI answers for Orient's target prompts. Understand the citation gap. Build content that closes it — by providing more authoritative, structured, and cited answers than current sources.
04
GEO Content Execution
11 articles across May–August, each written to GEO standards: cited statistics in the first 300 words, clear H2 structure answering sub-questions, FAQ schema, and internal links to core landing pages.

2
A replicable citation formula
Phase 1 produces a documented understanding of what structure, claim type, and source format causes AI to select a page as a citation source. This formula is a permanent content asset that applies to all future content in Q4 and beyond.

AI citation typically has a 60–90 day lag from publish to first appearance. Articles published in July and August will continue accumulating citation traction into Q4. These articles remain on the site indefinitely and are stronger content assets than what currently exists — for both SEO and AIO.
